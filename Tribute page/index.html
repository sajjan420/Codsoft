About Shivaji Maharaj

Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj (1630–1680) was the founder of the Maratha Empire in western India. Revered as a legendary warrior king, he is known for his progressive leadership, innovative military tactics, and unwavering dedication to his people and land. Shivaji Maharaj’s vision and valor continue to inspire millions across generations.

"Freedom is a boon, which everyone has the right to receive."
— Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj

Admirable Qualities

  • Courageous Leader: Fearlessly fought against powerful empires to protect his people.
  • Innovative Strategist: Master of guerrilla warfare, fort architecture, and naval power.
  • Secular and Just: Respected all religions, promoted justice, and fostered harmony.
  • Visionary: Established a competent administration and welfare policies for his subjects.
  • Empowering: Encouraged the participation of women and common people in governance.

Legacy

Shivaji Maharaj’s legacy is celebrated for his commitment to freedom, self-rule (Swarajya), and good governance. His forts, navy, and code of ethics laid the foundation for the Maratha Empire. He remains a symbol of pride, unity, and inspiration for millions.
